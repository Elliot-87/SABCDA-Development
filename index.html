About SABCDA

Building South African aspiring entrepreneurs and small businesses to be corporate entities

Our Story

The infiltration of rural and township businesses by foreign competitors became a matter of great concern to South African citizens. Daily, business and job opportunities were being lost, while unemployment, poverty, and crime rates continued to rise.

Faced with these challenges and with little assistance from authorities, South African aspiring entrepreneurs, emerging small businesses, and community leaders under the banner of African Renaissance Chamber of Commerce and Industry, led by Masikule Business Consultancy, decided to take action.

This collective effort resulted in the development of the Rural, Township, Business and Community Development Model (RTBCDM), which is now being implemented across South Africa to counter these economic challenges and create sustainable opportunities.

Community Shops Establishment

Developing local spazza shops into sustainable businesses that serve as distribution points for quality products while preserving the small business space for future generations.

  • Twelve ward spazza shops developed as agents and distributors
  • Access to Zenzele Wholesale for bulk buying
  • Business mentorship and training programs

Implementation Process

Our Community Shops program follows a structured 6-month implementation process:

  1. Community needs assessment and shop selection
  2. Business registration and compliance setup
  3. Inventory management system implementation
  4. Owner training and mentorship program
  5. Supply chain integration with Zenzele Wholesale
  6. Ongoing monitoring and support

50 New Spazza Shops Launched in Gauteng

May 10, 2025 • Success Story

Community Enterprise Creates 30 Jobs in Eastern Cape

A community-owned enterprise supported by SABCDA has created 30 sustainable jobs in a rural Eastern Cape community...

Success Story Details

The Mthatha Community Food Hub, established with support from SABCDA's Community Owned Enterprise Program, has become a thriving business that employs 30 local residents. The hub combines a grocery store, bakery, and catering service, serving over 500 families in the area.

Impact Achieved:

  • 30 full-time jobs created (60% women, 40% youth)
  • R1.2 million in annual revenue in first year
  • 15% of profits reinvested in community projects

"This enterprise has transformed our community," says manager Nosipho Dlamini. "We're not just a business - we're a hub for economic activity and skills development."
